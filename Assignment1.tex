\section{Problem}
If A and B are two events such that P(A)=$\frac{1}{4}$,P(B)=$\frac{1}{2}$ and P(AB)=$\frac{1}{8}$.find P(not A and not B).
\section{Solution}
P(not A and not B) is equivalent to $P(A^\prime B^\prime)$.\\
from De-morgan's law,\\
\begin{align}
    (A^\prime B^\prime)=(A+B)^\prime\\
\end{align}
    So, $P(A^\prime B^\prime)=P((AB)^\prime)$
\begin{align}
  P((A+B)^\prime)=1-P(AB)\\
  =1-(P(A)+P(B)-P(AB))\\
  =1-\brak{\frac{1}{4} +\frac{1}{2} -\frac{1}{8}}\\
  =\frac{3}{8}\\
  \end{align}
  Therefore,\\
 \begin{align}
  P((A+B)^\prime)=\frac{3}{8}\\
  \Rightarrow P(A^\prime B^\prime)=\frac{3}{8}\\
  \end{align}
  So, P(not A and not B)=$\frac{3}{8}$\\

      \centering
      \includegraphics[width=11cm]{figure1.jpg}
  \end{figure}\\
  Here the sky blue colored region is the required and that is P(not A and not B)
